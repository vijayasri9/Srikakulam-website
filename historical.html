Salihundam is an ancient Buddhist site located on the banks of the Vamsadhara River in Srikakulam district. The site was discovered in 1919 and dates from the 2nd to 12th century CE, reflecting Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana Buddhism. It features four stupas, chaityas, viharas, relic caskets, and sculptures of deities like Mareechi and Tara. Salihundam played a key role in the spread of Buddhism to Southeast Asian countries like Sumatra and Thailand. The site is surrounded by scenic paddy fields and is a major heritage and tourist attraction in Andhra Pradesh.
