Ratha Saptami is a Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Surya, celebrated on the seventh day (Saptami) of the bright half of the Magha month.It marks the symbolic birth of the Sun God and the beginning of his northward journey, known as Uttarayana.Devotees take a ritual bath at sunrise, placing Arka leaves on the body, believed to cleanse sins and promote good health.Special prayers, Aditya Hridayam chanting, and offerings like paramannam (sweet rice) are made to honor the Sun.The day is also associated with agricultural prosperity and is considered highly auspicious for spiritual growth and healing.

The Kothamma Thalli Jathara in Kotabommali, Srikakulam, is a vibrant and deeply revered festival celebrated annually from October 1st to 3rd, now officially recognized as a state festival by the Andhra Pradesh government.Devotees from Andhra Pradesh, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and Jharkhand flock to the temple to offer prayers and fulfill vows to Kothamma Thalli, the guardian goddess of the region. The festivities include special rituals, cultural performances, and a grand procession, with the temple beautifully illuminated and adorned for the occasion. The legend behind the temple speaks of a divine encounter in the early 20th century, where the goddess revealed herself to a devotee, leading to the establishment of the Kothamma Thalli temple as a powerful spiritual center.Devotees offer silken garments, turmeric pots, and traditional gifts, believing the goddess grants wishes and protects her devotees like a Kalpavalli‚Äîa wish-fulfilling divine mother
